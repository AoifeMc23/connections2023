ARTIST STATEMENT

Aoife McNamara is a Videographer, Photographer and Visual Artist from Cork, Ireland in the early stages of her artist career. As a recent graduate of Creative Digital Media BA (Hons) from Munster Technological University, she has honed her skills in video editing and filmmaking through coursework and also through her professional work and freelance experience, becoming advanced in software such as Adobe Premiere Pro, Adobe Photoshop and Adobe After Effects.

With a passion for visual art and working with projectors and interactive technologies, she connected her knowledge and skills from her degree to explore using these technologies in an interactive installation.

Aoife’s latest project, “connections”, focuses on engaging the audience with technology and each other in a shared space. Audience members are invited into the installation where their audio data and hand movements are transformed into dynamic and colourful visual imagery that are projected onto the walls and floor. The visual output directly correlates to the audience size and volume levels. The methodologies behind her work involves an iterative process through thorough experiments where she explores the limits of her skills working different softwares and devices to create an interactive experience for the audience.

PROJECT PROPOSAL SUMMARY

This project “connections”, is an interactive art installation that is generated by the audience's presence. The project aims to highlight the idea that our once social spaces are changing and becoming places of social collection, where people come together but do not speak to each other. (Turkle, 2011, p.155) By using the audience's presence and interactions it aims to highlight the idea of using interactive technology as a collective rather than an individual.

Each audience member is invited to step into the installation both in the physical environment and the visual outputs. It uses interactive technologies to collectively engage the audience in a shared art piece where the audience’s input is the key factor. Without the audience, the piece doesn’t show and the more audience members there are, the more reactive and engaging the installation becomes.

The installation will contain two main elements that capture the audience's interactions and physical movements. Their audio data will be transformed into visuals that are projected onto the floor and the hand movements are tracked and projected onto the wall.

This proposal is for the production of this installation in its full form that aims to be displayed in three art galleries in Ireland in 2024. The amount that is being requested is: €17731.64

DETAILS OF PROPOSAL

BACKGROUND

Connections - “the state of being related to someone or something else” - Cambridge Dictionary

“For hundreds of thousands of years, humans only communicated in person” and it is only just in the past fifty years we have started communicating online. (Lieberman and Schroeder, 2020, p16) Communication is one of “the most important factors shaping human destiny,” however, our once communal spaces such as a cafe or the park have become spaces of “social collection” where “people come together but do not speak to each other.” (McFarlane, 2010, p.1; Turkle, 2012, p.155) This shift in our society has created an imbalance where we are still learning about the impacts that technology has on our in person interactions with each other.

“Technology should be enhancing our connection to others, not replacing it.” - (CMHA, 2019)

Digital technology in our lives has proven to be excellent in many different ways. Alongside social media it provided an outlet for adolescents during COVID-19, which was fundamental to help youth overcome the “stressful period and to limit psychological adverse events related to the lockdown.” (Salzano et al., 2021) However, despite its amazing qualities, in our personal relationships and in our offline connections with others it has been shown that it can have a negative effect on our relationships. (McDaniel and Coyne, 2016)

Recognising this shift in our society, this project is an interactive environment that is displayed in a shared environment where the audience is invited to interact both with each other and the piece itself. It aims to give the audience a space to explore ways in which interactive, digital technology can be used in a shared environment, where the audience collectively contributes to the visual output. Using visual arts as a medium, it uses software to transform the audience's movement and audio data to create the visuals for the piece which are then projected onto the floor and the wall using two projectors.

TECHNICAL DETAILS

There are two main elements of interactive graphics that are produced in this installation. The first is the audience’s audio input. The audience’s audio levels are captured through microphones around the room and brought into the software Touchdesigner. In real time, the audio input is collected and used to create graphics that are then projected onto the floor.

Video of colourful visuals that are influenced from the audience's audio input

The second is the audience’s physical bodies, specifically their hands. Using an Xbox Kinect, their right hand’s movement is tracked and projected directly onto the wall where each audience member can control their own circular bubble. When their bubbles overlap they interact and merge together creating new colors and forms.

Video of colourful and dynamic visuals that are influenced by the audience's hand movements

The project uses Touchdesigner to create the graphics with the audience's input coming from Xbox Kinect’s v2 and microphones. The interactive graphics are then projected on one wall and the floor where the audience can interact with the project and immediately see the results of their connections with each other.
